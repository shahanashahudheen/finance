Car Loan & Commercial Vehicle Loan

A car loan is a type of loan provided to help you purchase a car. Car loans follow most of the same rules and procedures that apply to other loans. In most cases, when purchasing a car, a borrower specifically applies for a car loan. These loans are typically offered for fixed tenures, generally between 24 and 60 months. Car loans usually include various fees and taxes, which are added to the total loan amount.

Commercial vehicle loans are loans offered to borrowers — usually self-employed individuals, trusts, partnership firms, or organizations — for the purchase of vehicles used for commercial or business purposes. These loans are often availed by those involved in the transportation business.

Key Features

  • Specially for car and commercial vehicle buyers
  • No security required
  • Low EMI
  • Fulfil your small and urgent needs
  • Low processing fee
  • 85% funding
  • Simple documentation
Commercial Loan

A commercial loan is a type of loan generally taken to purchase commercial buildings or land, or to construct commercial properties for future income generation. It is also a type of mortgage loan, meaning the property documents are mortgaged with the bank.

Key Features

  • Maximum tenure 20 years
  • Maximum funding
  • Floating rate scheme
  • Escrow account option
  • Low interest rate
  • Customized EMI
Project Loan

Project finance is the long-term financing of infrastructure and industrial projects based on the projected cash flows of the project rather than the balance sheets of its sponsors. These loans are most commonly non-recourse, meaning they are secured by the project assets and repaid entirely from the project’s cash flow, rather than from the general assets or creditworthiness of the project sponsors. The financing is typically secured by all project assets, including revenue-producing contracts.

Key Features

  • Capital Intensive Financing Scheme
  • Risk Allocation
  • Zero or Limited Recourse Financing Solution
  • Loan Repayment With Project Cash Flow
  • Multiple Participants Applicable
  • Better Tax Treatment
Takeover & Top-up Loan

A takeover loan is a type of loan that allows you to transfer the balance amount of an existing loan from one financial institution to another to benefit from a lower interest rate or to add more funds to the loan. It also retains all applicable government subsidies associated with the original loan.

A top-up loan allows existing customers to borrow additional funds over and above their existing loan. The top-up functions as a new loan that completely settles the existing one, with any remaining balance disbursed to the borrower. Tax deduction benefits are available on the interest portion of the top-up loan under Section 24 of the Income Tax Act. A top-up loan can be taken on an existing home loan based on income and repayment capacity, and most banks offer such loans readily to their existing customers.

This loan option helps borrowers transfer existing loans for lower interest rates, avail additional funding, retain government subsidies, enjoy tax benefits, and experience quick processing with flexible repayment options.
